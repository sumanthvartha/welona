02 — What are they feeling?
The emotional rollercoaster
nobody talks about.

Seeking a cosmetic or medical treatment is not a rational purchase. It's an emotional journey that starts with shame and ends — if we do our job — with confidence.

😔

1. The Private Struggle

SHAME

They notice the problem but don't talk about it. Hair in the shower drain. Mirror avoidance. Avoiding certain clothes. The problem is invisible to others but LOUD in their head.

"Maybe it's normal. Maybe I'm overreacting. I'll just wait and see."
Duration: Weeks to months. Action: None — just worrying.
🔍

2. The Midnight Research

CONFUSION

Something triggers action — a comment, a photo, a friend's result. They open Google at 11 PM. PRP? GFC? Transplant? Laser? CoolSculpting? Every website says "best results." Nothing is clear. Every clinic looks the same.

"There are 50 clinics in Chennai. They all say the same thing. How do I know which one is real?"
Duration: Days to weeks. Action: 15-30 Google searches. 5-8 clinic websites. Reddit. YouTube.
😰

3. The Fear Spiral

FEAR

They find a treatment that sounds right but then Google the side effects. Horror stories on forums. "PRP made my hair worse." "Laser burned my face." The fear of a bad outcome is now LOUDER than the original problem.

"What if it doesn't work? What if it makes it WORSE? That's money wasted AND damage done."
Duration: Days. Action: Searches for side effects, horror stories, negative reviews. This is where most users DROP OFF.
🤔

4. The Price Shock

ANXIETY

If they survive the fear stage, they hit the price wall. No clinic shows prices. "Contact us for pricing." They have NO IDEA if it's ₹5,000 or ₹5,00,000. They can't even decide if it's worth exploring without knowing the range.

"I don't want to call and then find out it's ₹2 lakhs. I'll look stupid. Just tell me the price."
Duration: This is a WALL. Many users stop here permanently. Action: Searches "[treatment] cost Chennai." Finds nothing useful.
🙋

5. The Trust Search

HOPE

They look for proof. Not from the clinic — from real people. "Did anyone from Chennai do PRP? What was your experience?" They want to find someone EXACTLY LIKE THEM who got results.

"Show me someone my age, with my problem, in my city, who actually got better."
Duration: Days. Action: Google reviews, YouTube testimonials, Reddit, asking friends. This is where VIDEO TESTIMONIALS convert.
✅

6. The Commitment Moment

DECISION

They've decided to try. But now they need to BOOK. And the clinic says "fill this form, we'll call back in 24-48 hours." In that waiting time, doubt creeps back in. They visit a competitor. The moment is lost.

"I was ready to book. But they made me wait for a callback. By then I'd already found another clinic."
Duration: Minutes. Action: Needs INSTANT booking — live slots, OTP confirmation. Any friction = lost patient.
"The user doesn't buy a treatment. They buy relief from the anxiety of not knowing what to do."
03 — What's hurting them?
Five pain points,
ranked by severity.

1. "I don't know what treatment I need"

CRITICAL

They know their PROBLEM (hair falling, skin scarring, fat not going) but not the SOLUTION. The clinic website lists treatments they've never heard of. PRP? GFC? FUE? They don't speak doctor.

Severity: 95/100 · Affects: Every new visitor · Current solution: None

2. "I'm scared of what could go wrong"

HIGH

Side effects are their #1 Google search BEFORE they search for clinics. "PRP side effects" gets more searches than "PRP clinics Chennai." No clinic addresses this. The silence feels like hiding something.

Severity: 90/100 · Affects: 70%+ of researchers · Current solution: None (clinics hide this)

3. "Nobody will tell me the price"

HIGH

They can't plan, can't budget, can't get family buy-in without even a ballpark. The "contact us for pricing" model exists because clinics want to upsell in person. But in 2026, price opacity = instant bounce.

Severity: 88/100 · Affects: Every visitor who doesn't call · Current solution: None

4. "Will this actually work for ME?"

MEDIUM

Before-after photos on clinic sites feel curated and fake. They want proof from someone who looks like them — same age, gender, skin tone, city, concern. Generic testimonials don't convert. Specific ones do.

Severity: 82/100 · Affects: Users at decision stage · Current solution: Screenshot images (look fake)

5. "I have to CALL? And WAIT?"

MEDIUM

They're ready to book at 11 PM on a Sunday. The clinic's form asks for name + phone. Then... nothing. A callback in 24-48 hours. By then they've either lost motivation, found another clinic, or talked themselves out of it.

Severity: 75/100 · Affects: All ready-to-book users · Current solution: Lead form → callback

06 — What makes them trust?
Trust is earned in layers.

Each layer of trust removes a specific fear. Skip a layer and the user drops off.

1️⃣

Layer 1: Social proof from people like me

Removes: "Does this work?"

Video testimonials from someone their age, with their concern, in their city. Not generic before-after photos — REAL people telling REAL stories with REAL timelines. This is the single most powerful trust signal in healthcare.

2️⃣

Layer 2: Transparent side effects

Removes: "Are they hiding something?"

When a clinic proactively shows side effects — including when a treatment WON'T work — the user thinks: "If they're honest about the bad stuff, the good stuff must be true too." Honesty is the most underused trust lever in Indian healthcare.

3️⃣

Layer 3: Transparent pricing

Removes: "Will I be upsold?"

Showing price ranges — even approximate — says "we respect your time and intelligence." A cost estimator with a session slider does more for trust than 50 before-after photos.

4️⃣

Layer 4: Doctor credentials

Removes: "Is the doctor qualified?"

Name, photo, qualification, years of experience, number of procedures done, Google rating, languages spoken. The absence of doctor profiles on a medical clinic website is a MASSIVE red flag for informed users.

5️⃣

Layer 5: Instant booking

Removes: "Will I have to chase them?"

Live slot availability + instant OTP confirmation = "this clinic has their act together." A callback model in 2026 says "we're disorganized" even if you're not. Self-service booking is now baseline expectation, not innovation.

08 — What job are they hiring us for?
They're not buying a treatment.
They're buying a feeling.
🧭

Job 1: "Help me understand my options"

"I know my hair is thinning. I don't know what PRP, GFC, or transplant means. Tell me in my language."

→ Solved by: Treatment quiz, comparison tool, plain-language guides
🛡️

Job 2: "Help me feel safe"

"I need to know what could go wrong, how likely it is, and what happens if it does. Don't hide things from me."

→ Solved by: Transparent side effects, safety scores, eligibility checker, "when it won't work"
💰

Job 3: "Help me plan financially"

"I need a number — even a range — so I can talk to my family and plan. Don't make me call to find out."

→ Solved by: Interactive cost estimator, transparent price ranges, EMI info
🪞

Job 4: "Show me someone like me"

"I don't want a model. I want a 32-year-old guy from Chennai who had the same hair problem and fixed it."

→ Solved by: Video testimonials, patient stories filtered by concern/age/location, peer consultation
⚡

Job 5: "Let me act on my decision NOW"

"I've decided. Don't make me fill a form and wait. Let me pick a time, confirm with OTP, and move on with my night."

→ Solved by: Live slot booking, OTP verification, instant WhatsApp confirmation
09 — The moments that matter
Five seconds where we
win or lose the patient.

Moment 1: The First 3 Seconds

MAKE OR BREAK

User lands from Google Ad. In 3 seconds they decide: "Is this for me?" If they see a generic hero banner with "Best Clinic in Chennai" — bounce. If they see a VIDEO of someone like them talking about their result — they stay.

Design implication: The center of the homepage MUST be real people with real stories. Not marketing. Not features. People.

Moment 2: The "How Much?" Moment

HIGH IMPACT

They've found a treatment that interests them. They look for the price. If it's hidden → they leave, feeling disrespected. If it's shown clearly with a range and a session slider → they feel empowered.

Design implication: Price should be on EVERY treatment card, not buried in a subpage. The cost estimator should be interactive and immediately visible.

Moment 3: The Side Effect Search

TRUST BUILDER

They Google "[treatment] side effects" after visiting your site. If your site already showed them — with honesty, categorized by severity — they come BACK. If it didn't, they found the answer on Reddit and now trust Reddit more than you.

Design implication: Side effects should be proactive, not hidden behind a toggle. Show safety scores on treatment cards themselves.

Moment 4: The "Book Now" Window

CONVERSION

They're ready. This window lasts MINUTES, not hours. If they can pick a slot and confirm in 90 seconds — you win. If they have to fill a form and wait for a callback — the window closes. It may never open again.

Design implication: Booking must be self-service, real-time, and complete in under 2 minutes. OTP confirmation, not callback.
